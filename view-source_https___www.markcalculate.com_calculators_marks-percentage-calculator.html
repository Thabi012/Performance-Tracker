<h2 class="orange">How to calculate the Percentage of a Number?</h2>
<p>
Generally, the numbers that are to be converted into percentage are given in two formats.
</p>
<div class="col-md-12 index-wrap">
<ul class="nav1 mb-0">
<li class="mb-3">
Decimal form – If the number is given in decimal format, it becomes relatively easy to find the percentage. To obtain the percentage, we simple have to multiply the given number by 100.<br>
<strong>Example:</strong> the given number is 0.45. To convert into a percentage, 0.45 * 100 = 45%
</li>
<li class="mb-3">
Fraction form: If the number given is in the fractional form, we first convert it into decimal value by dividing the top value by the total value and then multiply with 100.
</li>

</ul>
</div>

By using this percentage formula, it becomes easy to know how to calculate the percentage of marks, discount etc.

<p>
Total students in the class=150<br>
Boys in the class = 75<br>
Percentage of the boys in the class = (75 ⁄ 150) × 100 = (7500 ⁄ 150) = 50%

</p>

<p>

<strong> For example: </strong>suppose 3 students took a test and received scores of 69, 87, and 92,


</p>

<h2 class="orange"> Comparing quantities using percentage </h2>
<p>
While comparing quantities and marks , the easiest way to do so is to convert all the figures into one common unit.
Percentage happens to be the perfect fit here because all quantities can be converted into percentage and comparing
becomes much easy and practical. Let’s take an example to understand this topic better.
</p>

<p>
<strong> Example:</strong> let store A sell 200 t shirts out of 400 and store B sell 30 pairs of shoes out of 50. Who had more sales?
</p>

<p>
<strong> Solution:</strong> as we can see here, the quantities are different and there is no other way to compare them other
than converting them into percentage.
</p>

<p>
Percentage of A = 200/ 400 * 100 </br>
= 0.5*100 </br>
= 50%
</p>

<p>
Percentage of B= 30/50 *100 </br>
= 0.6* 100 </br>
= 60%
</p>

<p>We can easily see that the store B had more sales than store A. </p>
<p>
<strong> Example: </strong> suppose Ria scored 39 out of 50 in maths and 58 out of 70 in physics. Where did she score better?
</p>

<p>
<strong> Solution: </strong> percentage of marks scored in maths= 39/ 50*100</br>
= 0.78*100 </br>
= 78%
</p>
<p>
Percentage of marks scored in physics = 58/70*100 </br>
=0.82*100 </br>
=82%
</p>

<h2 class="orange"> What is the Difference between Percentage & Fraction? </h2>
<p>
Percentage and fractions are very closely related but have a major difference. A fraction is when a 2 units are divided
and a resultant represents a part of the whole. Meanwhile, percentage is when the fraction is multiplied by 100.
</p>
<p>
<strong> Example: </strong> percentage of marks scored in maths= 39/ 50*100 </br>
= 0.78 </br>
39/50 is a fraction whose resultant is 0.78 which is a part of the whole. </br>
When we multiply this 0.78 by 100 we get 78% .

</p>
